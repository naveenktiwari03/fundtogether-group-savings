# Product Brief: FundTogether

## Problem

Group saving is common, but the tools are informal. People coordinate through
chat messages, manual transfers, screenshots, spreadsheets, and one trusted
organizer. That creates friction around:

- who has paid
- how close the group is to the goal
- whether money is being held safely
- what happens when someone drops out
- who approves withdrawals
- how progress is communicated without constant reminders

The emotional problem is trust. The operational problem is coordination.

## Target User

Primary user:

- organizes shared financial goals for friends, family, or a community group
- currently tracks contributions manually
- wants visibility without becoming a full-time treasurer
- needs an easy way to keep people aligned after the group agrees on a goal

Secondary user:

- contributes to shared goals
- wants confidence that their contribution is recorded
- wants reminders and progress updates without chasing the organizer
- needs to understand where the group stands before contributing again

## Product Thesis

The valuable wedge is a transparent shared ledger around a goal.

```text
goal creation -> member invites -> contribution tracking -> milestone progress
```

The product does not need to begin as a full investment platform. It can first
prove that groups want a trusted system of record for shared savings.

## MVP Scope

The current prototype focuses on:

- clear value proposition
- goal creation
- dashboard of active funds
- fund-level progress
- contributor visibility
- contribution and activity history
- risk preference concept for future financial routing

## Non-Goals For The Prototype

- real payments
- real KYC
- actual investment execution
- withdrawal settlement
- dispute resolution
- tax reporting
- production compliance controls

## Discovery Questions

- Who starts most shared savings goals: the payer, the planner, or the most
  trusted member of the group?
- What makes contributors comfortable sending money to a shared pool?
- Would users prefer a lightweight tracker before connecting payments?
- Which goals are most urgent: travel, gifts, emergencies, family events, or
  community projects?
- What withdrawal approval model feels fair?
- At what amount does the group need more formal trust and permission controls?

## Success Criteria

An early product test would be promising if:

1. organizers can create a fund in under two minutes
2. invited members understand the goal and contribution expectations
3. at least three members join a fund without manual explanation
4. members return to check progress
5. users ask for payment integration after using the tracking workflow

## Risks

- Real-money pooling introduces regulatory and trust complexity.
- Investment language could create unrealistic expectations.
- Group dynamics are messy; permission and withdrawal rules need careful design.
- Users may prefer existing payment apps unless the shared ledger creates clear
  incremental value.

## Product Takeaways

This prototype separates a broad fintech idea into testable product layers:

- prove the coordination problem first
- validate trust and transparency needs
- introduce money movement only after workflow demand is clear
- define metrics that connect user behavior to product risk
